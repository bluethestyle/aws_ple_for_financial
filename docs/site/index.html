The Problem We Solve

Current State

  • Recommendation models output probabilities — customers see no reason to trust them
  • SHAP/LIME explanations are unstable and say "feature_237 = 0.12" — meaningless to a banker
  • Multiple models (churn + recommendation + segment) = multiple pipelines to manage
  • Regulators (Korean FSS, EU AI Act) demand explanations the current stack can't provide
  • GPU servers are expensive to maintain for sporadic training needs

Our Solution

  • 7 named experts that each capture a different "why" — gate weights are the explanation
  • "35% spending trend + 28% product hierarchy fit" — business language, no post-hoc tools
  • One model, 13 tasks, one config file — single management point
  • Built-in regulatory mapping: FSS guidelines, EU AI Act, Korean AI Basic Act
  • Distill to LGBM, serve on Lambda — no GPU needed for inference

7 Experts, 7 Perspectives

Each expert sees the same customer through a different scientific lens.

DeepFM
Feature Interaction
Captures cross-effects: income x product holdings x channel preference. FM computes 2nd-order interactions in O(nk).
Why: Financial behavior depends on feature combinations, not individual features alone.
Temporal Ensemble
Mamba + LNN + Transformer
Three temporal models for three time scales: long-range trends (Mamba), disrupted sequences (LNN), short-range context (Transformer).
Why: Financial time series mix monthly snapshots, daily transactions, and multi-month dormancy gaps.
Hyperbolic GCN
Tree Hierarchy
Embeds the MCC product category tree in 8D Poincare ball — exponentially less distortion than Euclidean space.
Why: "Savings deposit" is closer to "time deposit" than to "mortgage" — hierarchy matters.
PersLay (TDA)
Topological Persistence
Detects spending pattern shapes: connected clusters (H0), consumption cycles (H1), systematic voids (H2).
Why: "Your spending has a persistent cyclic pattern" is an explanation no other feature type can produce.
LightGCN
Graph Convolution
Collaborative filtering on the customer-product bipartite graph. Stripped to essentials — just neighborhood aggregation.
Why: "Customers with similar portfolios also hold this product" — the classic collaborative signal.
Causal (NOTEARS)
DAG Constraint
Learns causal direction between features via continuous DAG optimization. Enables "A causes B" not just "A correlates with B."
Why: Regulators and customers demand causal reasoning, not just statistical association (Pearl, 2009).
Optimal Transport
Distribution Matching
Sinkhorn-regularized Wasserstein distance between customer profiles and segment prototypes.
Why: Measures how far a customer is moving between segments — direction and distance, not just membership.

Regulatory Compliance by Design

Not bolted on after deployment — embedded in the architecture.

Explainability
Expert gate weights + 3-agent LLM reason generation. Every recommendation has a traceable justification.
Fairness
Disparate Impact, Statistical Parity, Equal Opportunity monitoring across 5 protected attributes.
Human Oversight
Sampling review, kill switch, AI opt-out. Champion-Challenger requires human sign-off.
Audit Trail
HMAC hash-chain immutable logs. 7 audit tables. Every decision reconstructible.
Drift Detection
PSI continuous monitoring. 3-day threshold triggers automatic retraining alert.
Incident Response
Severity-based classification. Automatic governance reports (monthly/quarterly).
